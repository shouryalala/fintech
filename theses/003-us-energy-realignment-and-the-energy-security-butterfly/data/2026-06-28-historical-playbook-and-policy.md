## Q1 — The chokepoint/embargo playbook: real, repeatable, but SLOW
| Shock | Structural response | Speed of *policy* reflex | Speed of *flow* shift |
|------|--------------------|--------------------------|------------------------|
| 1973 Arab embargo | IEA created (1974), SPR, CAFE efficiency standards ✓3-0 | **~1 year** (IEA in 1974) | Efficiency multi-decade (US energy/GDP <½ of 1970s by 2013) ✓3-0 |

🟡 **Read:** The pattern is genuine and recurring — but the **policy/military reflex is fast (months–~1yr) while the durable *flow* re-channeling is slow (3–10yr).** Best modern base rate (2022→US LNG) ≈ 3–4 years. **The timeline itself is the under-appreciated variable** — this is a multi-year, not a quarters-long, thesis.

## Q3 — US (+ allied) energy-security policy actually in motion (all 3-0 unless noted)
- **LNG exports unleashed:** Biden export pause lifted via **E.O. 14154 (20 Jan 2025)**; DOE restarted non-FTA reviews (Wright order 5 Feb 2025); Commonwealth LNG conditional auth 14 Feb 2025. DOE claims it has approved more LNG capacity than the #2 exporter ships (self-assessment).
- **Nuclear:** four **nuclear E.O.s (23 May 2025)** — national goal **~100 GW → 400 GW by 2050**, 10 large reactors under construction by 2030, streamlined NRC licensing.
- **Reactor restarts (federal loans):** ~$1.52B Holtec/Palisades (MI); **$1B closed Nov 2025 for Crane/Three Mile Island Unit 1 (PA, 850 MW).**
- **SMR funding:** **$800M to TVA + Holtec (Dec 2025)**; TerraPower Natrium got the first NRC construction permit for a commercial non-LWR (Mar 2026).
- **Domestic uranium/enrichment:** **$2.7B DOE for domestic enrichment (Jan 2026), incl. $900M for LEU/HALEU.**
- **SPR:** refill is **tiny** — DOE awarded only **~1M bbl (Nov 2025)** vs the **180M bbl** 2022 drawdown; capacity authorized 714 MMbbl; SPR had fallen to **~331 MMbbl by late-June 2026** as the live IEA-coordinated release drained it.

⚠️ **Caveats:** Most of this is permits/awards/loans/groundbreakings — **multi-year lead times, not shipped molecules or megawatts.** And the Trump posture framed **chokepoint-flow protection as the *buyers'* responsibility, not a US guarantee** — a notable break from the 1980s reflagging precedent.

**Read:** The structural-response *playbook* is sound and the *policy* leg is real, dated, and government-administered (not rhetoric) — **the most under-appreciated *durable signal* in the thesis** — but it is long-fuse capex, so it argues for patience over a 1–5yr horizon, not a near-term volume surge.
