# Transmission mechanism & second-order effects — AI-equity correction → CA/SV real estate

**As-of:** 2026-07-07. All numbers cited from secondary/reporting sources (URLs in agent transcript).
Educational research only. Sourcing caveats flagged at the end.

## Causal chain (ordered): AI equity correction → Bay Area RE
1. **AI equity repricing** — AI/Mag-7 names sell off; Bay Area workers' RSU/option paper wealth marks down.
2. **Down-payment fuel dries up** — purchases are disproportionately equity-funded; jumbo underwriting
   counts 2-yr average RSU income, so a stock drop shrinks both the down-payment pool *and* qualifying income.
3. **VC + IPO spigot narrows** — SF absorbed **~$154B VC in 2025**; a derating cuts new-money creation.
4. **AI hiring/leasing reverses** — AI = **30% of SF office leasing since 2023, >75% of net absorption**;
   retrenchment re-opens the office glut and slows the wage engine.
5. **Data-center / CRE values wobble** — AI capex (~$660–725B in 2026) underpins DC demand; a capex cut
   hits absorption and the CRE debt on it.
6. **Regional-bank credit tightens** — CA banks carry the nation's highest CRE-to-capital; CRE stress +
   tech-deposit flight (the SVB channel) tightens local credit, amplifying the drawdown.
7. **Second-order (slow): property-tax base + migration** — slower reassessment; renewed high-earner out-migration.

**Net:** the 2024–26 recovery to record highs is *itself* AI-driven, so correlation of a correction to
RE is now HIGHER than in a diversified-tech regime — the same mechanism that pushed prices up reverses.

## What's structurally different vs 2000
1. **No rate cushion.** The single biggest difference — 2000's ~550bps offset is gone (3.50–3.75% start, sticky inflation).
2. **Supply is the shock absorber this time.** SF inventory ~1.2 months + Prop 13 lock-in → a demand shock hits *volume/transactions* first, prices only later. Strongest bull counterweight.
3. **AI-monoculture, not diversified dot-com.** The *entire* SF office recovery + marginal housing bid is AI-specific → current strength is fragile to a single-sector derating.
4. **A data-center CRE channel with no 2000 analog** — ~$450B/yr AI capex embedded in physical RE + debt.
5. **Regional-bank fragility is demonstrated (SVB 2023), not hypothetical** — but the Fed will backstop.
6. **2022–23 layoff experiment is the key tell against panic** — a real multi-year layoff wave produced only a shallow, rate-amplified dip that fully reversed. Layoffs *alone* are insufficient.

**Bottom line:** transmission is real and, vs 2000, arrives with *less* monetary cushion and a *new*
data-center CRE channel — but is *braked* by record-tight supply + Prop 13. Decisive variables:
(i) the Fed's room/willingness to cut into an AI shock; (ii) whether a correction is equity-only or
spreads to VC/hiring/CRE — the 2022–23 experiment shows layoffs in isolation don't crash this market.

**Sourcing caveats:** SF office vacancy varies by methodology (JLL ~32% vs Q2 ~29.7% vs CommercialCafe
23.3% May-2026). Data-center scenario numbers ($3.7T vs $5.2T) are an advisory-firm model, not official.
