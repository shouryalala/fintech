# Bay Area residential real estate — dot-com bust (1999–2004) vs AI boom (2025–2026)

**As-of:** 2026-07-07. Numbers cited from reporting/index sources (URLs in agent transcript).
Educational research only. Data-quality flags at the end.

## Part 1 — Dot-com bust housing (~1999–2004)

**Verdict: the "housing didn't crash like tech stocks" claim is CONFIRMED.** Nasdaq −78%
(5,048.62 Mar 10 2000 → ~1,114 Oct 2002); Bay Area *median* home values fell only low-single-digits
to ~7.5%, and lower/mid tiers barely moved. Pain concentrated in (1) high-end homes, (2) condos, (3) rents.

**Zillow ZHVI (housing peak = May 2001, ~14 months AFTER the Nasdaq's Mar-2000 peak):**

| Metric | San Jose | San Francisco | US |
|---|---|---|---|
| Median value change, May 2001→Jan 2002 | **−7.5%** | **−0.7%** | **+3.3%** (rose) |
| Top-third (high-end) drop | **−9.5%** | **−3.8%** | — |
| Bottom-third (low-end) drop | **−3.8%** | smaller | — |
| Median recovery to pre-bust | **28mo → Sep 2003** | **11mo → Mar 2002** | — |
| High-end recovery | **35mo → Apr 2004** | **15mo → Aug 2002** | — |

**Rents fell far harder than prices** (tracked tech employment directly): Central Bay rents peaked
Mar 2001 ($1,628); by 2002 SF −12% / Silicon Valley −18%; by 2003 SF −18% / **Silicon Valley −34%**.

**Job losses (peak-2000 → trough fall-2003):** Silicon Valley **−225,000 (−21%)**; SF+San Mateo
**−160,000 (−14%)**; East Bay −47,000 (−5%); total Bay **~434,000 (−13.4%)**. Paper wealth: Nasdaq
−78%; ~$7T of the $17.8T run-up gain vaporized.

**Why housing didn't crash:** (1) Fed cut aggressively (funds rate ~−37% in one year, + post-9/11) →
cheaper mortgages cushioned prices; (2) chronic supply constraint / low inventory floored prices;
(3) homeowners weren't forced sellers — low/mid tiers "shrugged off" the bust; market stayed flat 4–5yr.
**Pushback to the "no crash" narrative:** San Jose high-end took ~3yr to recover and rents fell
18–34% — there *was* a real multi-year dip at the top and in the rental economy.

## Part 2 — Today (2025–2026): the AI-money influx

"Strongest luxury market in 25 years"; brokers positioning for OpenAI/Anthropic IPOs expected before
end-2026 to mint thousands of millionaires.

**AI comp (the modern stock-option analogue):** frontier-lab ICs ~$385K mid → ~$610K staff → $1.0M+
principal; **OpenAI L5 ~$1.15M TC** ($336K base + $774K stock); reported avg stock comp ~$1.5M/employee
(~4,000 staff, late-2025, single-source). Anthropic median ~$420–600K. **Equity now 60–70% of TC**
(up from 35–45% two years prior); valuations up 4–8x since 2023 (Anthropic ~$61.5B+, OpenAI rumored ~$500B).

**All-cash & inventory:** Palo Alto **35.1% all-cash in 2025**; Bay luxury down payments ~35%
(peaked 38.3% in 2023); Atherton chronically **<10–15 active listings**, median DOM ~48d (from >150d);
well-priced Palo Alto homes sell in 8–10 days.

**Prices at records:** Palo Alto median **$3.8M in 2025** (+5.1% YoY, +15% vs 2023); homes ≥$8M were
6.4% of sales (record). Atherton median **>$7.4M (Feb 2026)** (figures volatile — tiny samples).

**Where the money concentrates:** Atherton, Palo Alto, Los Altos, Menlo Park, Woodside, Mountain View,
Marin. Examples: West Atherton teardown listed ~$8M → **14 offers → $9.5M** (Nov 2025); 57 Fairview
$7.89M → $10.7M (~+33% YoY); Potrero Hill SF $1.4M → **13 offers → $2.1M**. Broker predicts 2026
"best luxury year in a 24-year career."

## Then vs now — key differences

| Dimension | Dot-com (1999–2004) | AI boom (2025–2026) |
|---|---|---|
| Wealth vehicle | Public stock/options — liquid but **−78%** | **Pre-IPO** equity (PPUs/RSUs) — **illiquid until IPO** |
| Liquidity timing | Paper wealth evaporated *before* converting to homes | Big IPO realization **still ahead** — cash may be pre-positioning |
| Direction now | Bust unfolding | Still in **boom/melt-up**; records set |
| Interest rates | Fed *cutting* ~37%/yr — cushioned | High-rate era, but **~35% all-cash** buyers rate-insensitive |
| All-cash share | Not defining | **Defining feature** — insulates from mortgage rates |
| Supply | Chronically tight | **Still chronically tight** — the structural constant |
| Pain/gain concentration | High-end + condos + rents fell hardest | High-end (Atherton/PA/Los Altos) rising hardest |
| Home-price behavior | Median barely dipped; high-end −3.8 to −9.5%; slow recovery | Median + high-end both at records |

**Crux for the thesis:** in 2000 the equity wealth was *already liquid and then vaporized*; in 2026
the AI equity is *still locked up pre-IPO* — so the biggest cash conversion (and biggest bubble-risk)
is arguably **still ahead**, not behind.

## Data-quality flags
- Precise annual SF/San Jose *median-dollar* prices 1999–2005 don't cleanly cross-check (indices do;
  dollar medians don't). Use the % tables as load-bearing.
- Atherton "median" 2024–26 highly volatile (tiny samples; $7.4M–$25.2M by source/period).
- Single-source: OpenAI "$1.5M avg stock/employee"; SpaceX "4,000 new millionaires" — indicative.
- Comp data aggregates third-party sites (Levels.fyi, blogs), not filings.
- All 2026 IPO expectations are forward-looking/unconfirmed as of the pull date.
