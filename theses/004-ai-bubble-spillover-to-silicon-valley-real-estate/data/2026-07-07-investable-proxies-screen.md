## Best (imperfect) proxies, ranked toward "Bay Area / SV housing"

   with a genuine NorCal/SF-Bay apartment sleeve. Near 52-wk high on ~33x P/E → the "premium,
   supply-constrained coastal" narrative is already partly priced.
2. **REZ** — most residential-tilted ETF for basket diversification; diluted by healthcare/storage, thinly traded.
3. **AVB / EQR** — partial coastal-apartment exposure, more diversified away from the Bay.
4. **EWBC / WAL** — indirect *credit/lending* proxies for CA real estate (not price); EWBC more CA-anchored, +25% year.
5. **EQIX / DLR / BXP** — the *AI-and-tech-in-the-Bay* theme via commercial/data-center RE, not housing.
   EQIX (+32% 1y, ~70x) is the cleanest "AI real estate" bid.

## KEY LIMITATION
**There is no clean long or short on Silicon Valley single-family housing** — pure Bay Area SFH is
**un-securitized and illiquid**, not a listed instrument. Every ticker here is a *leaky proxy*:
- Apartment REITs (ESS/AVB/EQR) track *multifamily rents* across metros, not SFH appreciation.
- Builders (LEN/DHI/PHM/KBH) track *national new-construction margins + rates*; SV is built-out
  (no-new-supply — the opposite of a builder's market). KBH most CA-weighted but inland/affordable.
- Data-center/office (EQIX/DLR/BXP) are an *AI-capex / tech-tenant* play, orthogonal to residential.
- Banks (WAL/EWBC) express *credit risk on CA CRE/tech lending*, not home-price direction.
- ETFs (VNQ/XLRE/REZ) dilute any housing signal across the whole RE complex.

A **direct** SV-housing view needs private/OTC vehicles (SFR funds, home-price-index derivatives —
no SF-specific liquid contract exists, private RE partnerships), none captured by public tickers.
Treat this screen as a **thematic overlay basket, not a targeted instrument.**
