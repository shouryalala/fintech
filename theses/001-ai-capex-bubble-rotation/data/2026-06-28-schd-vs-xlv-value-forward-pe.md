## Read
- **Trailing→forward gap is the tell.** SCHD 18.9x → ~15.3x = low-growth value by design.
  XLV 25.9x → ~19.9x = market pricing ~30% earnings growth (pharma/biotech pipelines; LLY GLP-1).
  So XLV's premium is **partly justified by growth**; SCHD's cheapness is **by construction**.
- **SCHD is NOT a value trap** (Open Q#1 answered): cheap from value/yield screening, not broken
  earnings — dividends grown every year since 2011, earnings-backed. Genuine cheap value.
- **On pure value, SCHD > XLV:** cheaper trailing & forward, ~2x the yield, lower vol, no trap.
- **Caveats:** (1) not apples-to-apples — SCHD is diversified multi-sector value+income (~20%
  healthcare already); XLV is concentrated single-sector growth (LLY 16%, top-10 = 61%). The real
  trade-off is *cheap-slow-diversified (SCHD)* vs *dearer-growing-concentrated (XLV)*. (2) Neither
  is on sale today — both near 52wk highs, above 200d MA; SCHD is *fairly valued*, not *distressed*.
- **Synthesis:** SCHD = cheap diversified core; XLV = defensive growth tilt. Matches the shield read
  ([holdings snapshot](2026-06-28-etf-holdings-ai-overlap.md)).
