## Q2 — Priced in *enough*? Base premium YES, tail NO 🟡
- JPMorgan fundamental fair value **≈ $58–60 Brent** in an **oversupplied** market (supply outpacing demand absent the disruption). Brent **~$73 ⇒ ~22% / ~$13–15 geopolitical premium already embedded.** So oil is **not** un-priced — the disruption *is* in the price.
- What's *not* fully priced is the **re-closure tail**: with ~10 mbd still impaired and active re-escalation, the curve/skew price a *resolving* path, not a full second shock. Verified finding: **tail risk under-priced** (high confidence).
- 🔴 **Correction to the owner's framing:** "oil never repriced / isn't priced in" is false (it spiked +78% then faded to a still-elevated $73). The defensible version is narrower: *the tail is under-priced*, not *the disruption is ignored*.

## Q3 — Are reserves propping the price? Largely NO 🔴 (claim mostly refuted)
- US authorized **172M bbl** SPR release; IEA coordinated its **largest-ever** release (11 Mar 2026; member strategic stocks ~400–426 mb). SPR ~340 Mbbl, lowest since 1983.
- **But realized release is only ~0.5 mbd — one-third of the ~1.43 mbd planned (EIA).** A ~0.5 mbd cushion cannot mask a ~10 mbd disruption. Reserves are **softening, not masking** — the price sits at $73 mainly because of *partial reopening + soft/oversupplied fundamentals*, not a reserve-built dam.
- ⚠️ **The most thesis-supporting source was refuted.** Brookings ("buffers exhausted by ~mid-July 2026 → 7.1 mbd / ~16% crunch"; "flows down to 1.5–2.5 mbd") was **killed 0–3** by the verification panel. The "imminent crunch when reserves run dry" narrative did **not** survive.
- **Forward bullish nudge (real):** the SPR **refill/replace obligation from ~Nov 2026** adds future buy-side demand — a genuine, dated tailwind, distinct from the (weak) "suppression" claim.

## Q4 — Another shock? Elevated & live 🟢 (supports the owner)
- Prediction markets (Kalshi) assign **~21–39%** to re-closure/escalation over the window. Active triggers as of late June: US strikes 26–27 Jun, Iran retaliation 28 Jun, **uncleared mines**, enrichment/IAEA-inspection disputes (NPR 24 Jun). Slow normalization (ADNOC 2027) keeps the system fragile.
- 🟢 A 21–39% binary that would move oil violently is a **materially under-appreciated convex tail** — this is the part of the owner's intuition that holds.
